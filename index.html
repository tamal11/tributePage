    Timeline of Legend M.N. Larma's life:

  • 1939- Born in Chittagong Hill Tracts(CHT) of Bangladesh and belonged to the Chakma tribe.
  • 1963- Graduated from the Government College in Chittagong, Bangladesh.

    Helping to organise a Hill Tracts students conference and working for the rights of proper compensation of the people of the Hill Tracts displaced by the Kaptai Hydroelectric Project and uprooted from their homestead and villages.

    Was arrested on February 10, under the East Pakistan Public Security Ordinance by police .
  • 1965- Released after two years of incarceration on March 8. Worked to unite and politically organise the various ethnic groups and tribes of the Chittagong Hill Tracts.
  • 1968- Completed B.Ed. degree from the Comilla Teachers' Training College.
  • 1970- Completed "Law Degree" from Chittagong Law College, Chittagong.

    Elected to the East Pakistan Provincial Assembly as an independent candidate.
  • 1971- After the creation of Bangladesh, Larma sought the recognition of the rights of the people of the Hill Tracts and regional autonomy.

    He opposed the drafted Constitution of Bangladesh, which made official the primacy of Bengali culture and did not recognise the non-Bengali ethnic groups and tribal communities living in Bangladesh.

    Larma's demands were rejected by the country's founding leader Sheikh Mujibur Rahman, who reportedly said that the tribal peoples should adopt Bengali culture.
  • 1972- Larma continued to fight for the rights of the indigenous people and is quoted as saying:
    "Under no definition or logic can a Chakma be a Bengalee or a Bengalee be a Chakma."
    On February 15, 1972 Larma founded the Parbatya Chattagram Jana Samhati Samiti (PCJSS), seeking to build an organisation representing all the Jumma Peoples of the Hill Tracts.
  • 1973- Elected to the Jatiya Sangsad, the national legislature of Bangladesh as candidate of the PCJSS.

    When Larma's continued efforts to make the state recognise the rights of the Jumma peoples failed, Larma and the PCJSS began organising the Shanti Bahini (Peace Corps), an armed force operating in the Hill Tracts area.
  • 1977- Began attacking state force .
  • 1983- However, factionalism within the PCJS weakened Larma's standing and he was assassinated on November 10.
